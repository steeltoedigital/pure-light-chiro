How red light therapy works on the skin


Red light therapy works by utilizing specific wavelengths of red light to penetrate the skin at a cellular level. When these red light photons are absorbed by the cells, they stimulate the production of adenosine triphosphate (ATP), which is the energy source for all cellular processes. This increased ATP production helps to boost collagen and elastin production, resulting in firmer, smoother skin with improved tone and texture.


In addition to its benefits for skin health, red light therapy also has powerful pain-relieving properties. The red light helps to reduce inflammation and increase blood flow, which can help alleviate sore muscles, joint pain, and other forms of discomfort. Red light therapy has been shown to be effective for conditions such as arthritis, fibromyalgia, and even chronic back pain.


Overall, red light therapy offers a non-invasive and natural way to improve both skin health and pain relief. Its ability to stimulate cellular regeneration and reduce inflammation make it a versatile treatment option for a wide range of conditions.
